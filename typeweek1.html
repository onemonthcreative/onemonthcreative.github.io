Week 1 Review
Typography

Initial Thoughts

This first week has been bitter sweet. On the sweet side, I feel that I have learned more in one week about typography than I have in the past couple of years, and I really mean that. Not only have I had the chance to learn so much about typography, through intensive studying, reading and exercises,I have learned to look at every element of design in a completely different way in terms of contrast, proportion and relation. This of course, is still week one and is something that I will have to build on and refine as I go on, but it really does make a big difference to learn from the best and look at things from a different perspective. On the flip side of this wonderful new revelation, looking back at some of my old web work that makes heavy use of typography makes me cringe and makes me wish that I had started a project like this sooner. This week was broken into the following sections:


1. Typography 101.

The Typography 101 course taught by The Futur has been the cornerstone of this module. I managed to work through the majority of the video series this week which has proved to be incredibly insightful at a both a theoretical and practical level. I find it very interesting to listen to a seasoned veteran speak in so much detail about typography and how certain key rules can completely change a design and once these rules are learned through practice, can then be broken. What I enjoy most about this course is that it is delivered from a practitioners point of view and you are put to work straight away through both follow along exercises and DIY homework assignments.

3. Tracing The Masters.

I really loved this one! This was a really fun exercise where I would find a typographic poster from one of the greats, draw a grid over it and recreate the poster myself in Illustrator. I will continue this every week and for this week, the great in question was Josef Muller-Brockmann. The purpose of this is to observe, in detail, the design decisions made by this legend when laying out a poster. 4. Follow Along Training.

In Typography 101, there are recordings where Chris critiques a designers work on a Helvetica case study. Instead of passively watching this critique (which alone, was very interesting), I also followed along to recreate the case study, applying the improvement areas mentioned by Chris.

5. DIY Homework.

This was the most difficult part of my week. As part of Typography 101, Chris gives a 7 week homework assignment adopted from Art Center and the Basel School of design which challenges the student to design a magazine cover given some specific constraints. This weeks constraints where to design 10 variations of magazine cover using 1 typeface, one point size and one weight. I thought that I would breeze through this but the experience was frustrating, humbling and ultimately, very rewarding. It forces you to stick to the basics and not apply any âtricksâ. It is time quite consuming and requires a considerable amount of effort but from just a weeks experience, is probably the most important part of the module. It forces you to create something without tracing or watching some else do it.

In Closing.

I am delighted with how the first week has gone and feel much more comfortable working with the dreaded type than I had been before. Next week will be much of the same activities with some slight variations
