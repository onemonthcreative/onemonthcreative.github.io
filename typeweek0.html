Week 0
Typography

Getting started with typography.

Typography is said to be the cornerstone of any good design and having worked on UX Design for the past year, I can attest to this. When designing a user interface, or anything else for that matter, laying out type would make me quite nervous and I would never really have full confidence that I knew what I was doing. Sometimes it would work out and sometimes it wouldn’t, but I had no idea why in either case. I often find myself trying to make up for my lack of typography knowledge by trying to use some fancy tricks or gimmicks which again, rarely seem to work out.


As this section of One Month Creative will serve many of the upcoming modules, I have decided to break my rule of learning a topic in 1 month and decided to dedicate 6 weeks to this. I know, I know, the project is called ‘One Month Creative’ and this is going to take 6 weeks. This will be the only topic to take longer than one month and if you’re going to break the rules, then break them early on!
This module is going to use the following:

1.Typography 101.

Typography course created by Chris Do and the Futur team which can be found here - https://academy.thefutur.com/. This course looks as though it will suit me quite nicely as I tend to learn by doing, and in Typography 101 Chris gives a 7 week homework assignment that I will work on during the duration of the module.

2. Essential Reading.

I will also read legendary typography books “Thinking With Type” and “Stop Stealing Sheep And Learn How Type Works”. My hope is that these books can reinforce what I learn in the above course and serve as a valuable resource for my homework assignment.

3. Pratice, practice, practice.

In and effort to learn more about how letterforms are constructed, I will engage in a daily sketching and tracing practice of lettering in order to gain a greater appreciation and understanding of what I will be laying out on a computer.

I will document my progress during these 6 weeks as best as I can on a weekly basis, posting my thoughts and progress pictures of my work. At the end of this course, I hope to have designed a series of high quality posters that illustrates that typography can indeed be learned and is nothing to be scared of.
